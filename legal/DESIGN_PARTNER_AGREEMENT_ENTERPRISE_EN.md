## PREAMBLE

STOA develops a cloud-native open source API management platform ("STOA Platform") for enterprises seeking to modernize their API infrastructure.

The Partner Company wishes to evaluate STOA Platform in an enterprise context and contribute to its improvement through feedback.

In return, STOA offers early access to features, priority support, and preferential commercial terms.

The Parties agree as follows:

---

## PART I — CONFIDENTIALITY

### ARTICLE 1 — CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

#### 1.1 Definition

The following constitutes "**Confidential Information**":

- Non-public product roadmap
- Unannounced features under development
- Internal architecture not publicly documented
- Strategic and commercial discussions
- Internal benchmark and performance data
- Negotiated commercial terms
- Any information expressly designated as confidential

#### 1.2 Exclusions

The following is **NOT** considered confidential:

- Source code published under Apache 2.0 license
- Public documentation available at docs.gostoa.dev
- Publicly announced features
- Information already known to the Partner Company prior to signing
- Information that has become public without fault of the Partner Company

### ARTICLE 2 — CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

#### 2.1 General Obligation

The Partner Company agrees to:

- Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties
- Limit internal access to persons with a need to know
- Protect Confidential Information with the same degree of care as its own confidential information, and in any event with a reasonable degree of protection

#### 2.2 Extension to Employees

The Partner Company warrants that its employees, contractors, and advisors with access to Confidential Information are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this agreement.

#### 2.3 Duration of Obligations

Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of this agreement for **three (3) years**.

---

## PART II — NON-COMPETE

### ARTICLE 3 — NON-COMPETE COMMITMENT

#### 3.1 Prohibition

The Partner Company agrees not to use Confidential Information to develop, have developed, market, or contribute to a product competing with STOA Platform for **eighteen (18) months** from the date of signature.

#### 3.2 Definition of Competing Product

A "competing product" means any cloud-native API management platform incorporating:
- An MCP (Model Context Protocol) gateway
- A universal API contracts system (UAC)
- Multi-tenant architecture with native isolation

#### 3.3 Exclusion

This clause does **not** apply to:
- Use of public source code under Apache 2.0 license
- Use of STOA Platform in accordance with its license
- Development of plugins or extensions for STOA Platform

## PART III — FEEDBACK AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### ARTICLE 4 — FEEDBACK

#### 4.1 Feedback Commitment

The Partner Company agrees to provide regular feedback on:
- Platform usage
- Difficulties encountered
- Improvement suggestions
- Unmet business needs

#### 4.2 Feedback Format

Feedback shall be provided through:
- Monthly follow-up meetings (1 hour maximum)
- Dedicated communication channel (Slack, Discord, or email)
- Bug/feature tickets upon request

#### 5.1 Assignment of Rights

The Partner Company assigns to STOA, free of charge and exclusively, all intellectual property rights over suggestions, ideas, and improvements communicated under this agreement.

#### 5.2 Waiver

The Partner Company expressly waives any claim, compensation, or demand regarding the integration of its suggestions into STOA Platform.

#### 5.3 Retained Rights

The Partner Company retains all rights over:
- Its own code and developments
- Its data and content
- Its trademarks and distinctive signs

## PART V — DURATION AND COMMERCIAL TERMS

### ARTICLE 8 — PARTNERSHIP DURATION

#### 8.1 Initial Term

This agreement enters into force on the date of signature for an initial term of **six (6) months**.

#### 8.2 Renewal

The agreement is renewed by tacit renewal for successive six (6) month periods, unless terminated by either Party with **thirty (30) days** notice before expiration.

#### 8.3 Early Access

During the partnership, the Partner Company benefits from:
- Priority access to new beta features
- Dedicated support channel with 48 business hour response SLA
- Participation in roadmap sessions (quarterly)

### ARTICLE 9 — COMMERCIAL CONVERSION TERMS

#### 9.1 Design Partner Discount

If subscribing to a STOA Enterprise license within **six (6) months** of general availability (GA), the Partner Company benefits from a **twenty percent (20%)** discount on the first year list price.

#### 9.2 Support Priority

During the pilot phase, the Partner Company benefits from priority support at no additional cost, equivalent to the "Premium Support" level in the STOA catalog.

#### 9.3 No Commitment

This agreement does not constitute a purchase commitment. The Partner Company remains free not to subscribe to a commercial offer at the end of the partnership.

## PART VI — LIABILITY AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

### ARTICLE 10 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

#### 10.1 Disclaimer

STOA Platform is provided "as is" during the Design Partner period. STOA does not warrant:
- Absence of bugs or interruptions
- Fitness for a particular purpose
- Compatibility with the Partner Company's infrastructure

#### 10.2 Limitation

STOA's liability under this agreement is limited to direct damages and shall not exceed **five thousand euros (€5,000)**.

#### 10.3 Exclusion

Under no circumstances shall STOA be liable for indirect damages, data loss, business interruption, or loss of profits.

### ARTICLE 11 — TERMINATION

#### 11.1 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this agreement at any time with **thirty (30) days** written notice (email with read receipt or registered mail).

#### 11.2 Termination for Cause

In case of material breach by a Party, the other Party may terminate this agreement with immediate effect after formal notice remaining without effect for **fifteen (15) days**.

#### 11.3 Effects of Termination

Upon termination:
- Confidentiality and non-compete obligations survive according to their terms
- The Partner Company returns or destroys Confidential Information
- Trademark usage rights cease immediately

### ARTICLE 12 — FINAL PROVISIONS

#### 12.1 Governing Law

This agreement is governed by French law.

#### 12.2 Jurisdiction

Any dispute relating to the interpretation or performance of this agreement shall be submitted to the competent courts of Bordeaux, France, after an attempt at amicable resolution for thirty (30) days.

#### 12.3 Entire Agreement

This agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties on its subject matter and supersedes any prior agreement, discussion, or proposal.

#### 12.4 Amendments

Any amendment to this agreement must be made by written addendum signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

#### 12.5 Severability

If any clause of this agreement is declared null or unenforceable, the other clauses remain in force.

#### 12.6 Assignment

This agreement may not be assigned by either Party without prior written consent of the other Party, except in case of restructuring or sale of the relevant business.
